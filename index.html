Protocol Overview

This protocol addresses two primary scenarios of alien arrival on Earth:

Scenario 1: Crashed Ship

Indicators: Distress signals, uncontrolled descent, impact damage, potential injuries

Assumptions: Aliens may be in distress, refugees, fugitives, or experiencing technical failure

Primary Concerns: Immediate hazards, contamination, injury response, securing crash site

Scenario 2: Safe Landing

Indicators: Controlled descent, intact vessel, deliberate landing location selection

Assumptions: Intentional contact, potentially peaceful intent, prepared for interaction

Primary Concerns: Understanding intent, establishing communication, diplomatic protocols

Location Variables

Adjust response based on landing location:

  • Urban: High population density, infrastructure concerns, rapid response availability
  • Rural: Isolation, slower response times, easier containment
  • Coastal/Beach: Naval response, water contamination risks, tourism considerations
  • Forest: Fire hazards, difficult access, environmental impact
  • Desert: Extreme conditions, minimal infrastructure, remote location
  • Mountains: Difficult terrain, altitude considerations, limited access

Phase 1: Immediate Response (0-2 Hours)

üö® First Responder Actions

Initial Alert

CRITICAL COMMUNICATION TIP: When calling emergency services, report as "non-commercial aircraft with no identifying markings has crashed" to avoid being dismissed as a prank. Provide specific details only after responders are dispatched.

Information to Provide:

  • Precise location (GPS coordinates, longitude/latitude)
  • Time and date of incident
  • Description of craft (size, shape, condition)
  • Observable hazards (fire, smoke, unusual emissions)
  • Number of visible occupants (if any)
  • Specific landmarks or identifying features nearby
  • Current weather conditions

Emergency Services to Alert:

  • Local law enforcement
  • Fire department
  • Paramedics/EMS
  • Local military (Army, Air Force, Space Force)
  • Navy/Coast Guard (if coastal or at sea)

Airspace Control

  • Establish no-fly zone immediately
  • Ground all civilian aircraft, drones, and helicopters in vicinity
  • Prevent mid-air collisions or interference
  • Deploy air defense on standby (hold fire per rules of engagement)

Perimeter Security

Law Enforcement Responsibilities:

  • Form immediate perimeter around site (minimum 100m radius, adjust based on hazards)
  • Secure scene and prevent civilian access
  • DO NOT FIRE unless fired upon
  • Document all civilians present (witnesses, recordings)
  • Prevent souvenir hunters and debris collection
CIVILIAN HAZARD WARNING: Curious civilians will gather and film. Some may attempt to approach the site or collect debris. Broken parts may leak radiation, toxic gases, or unknown hazardous materials. Enforce perimeter strictly.

Fire and Hazard Response

Fire Department Actions:

  • Extinguish fires in surrounding area
  • Deploy HAZMAT teams with detection equipment
  • Be alert for radiation, toxic gases, or unknown fluids leaking from craft
  • Use drones for aerial reconnaissance to minimize human exposure
  • Establish exclusion zones based on contamination risk

Medical Response

Paramedic/EMS Protocols:

  • Priority: Treat injured humans only
  • DO NOT treat injured aliens if biological - risk of foreign bacteria/viruses
  • If aliens appear humanoid, wear full hazmat suits before approach
  • Establish decontamination stations for exposed humans
  • Document all human-alien contact incidents

Phase 2: Scenario-Specific Protocols (2-6 Hours)

Scenario 1: Crashed Ship - Additional Protocols

Assumptions:

  • Occupants may be injured or in distress
  • Potential refugees or fugitives
  • Ship may be damaged and unstable
  • Communication systems possibly impaired

Priority Actions:

  • Assess structural stability of craft
  • Determine if occupants are alive
  • Identify immediate life-threatening hazards
  • Prepare for potential rescue operation (with full contamination protocols)
  • Monitor for distress signals or communication attempts

Time-Sensitive Concerns:

  • Ship self-destruct mechanisms
  • Rapidly spreading contamination
  • Deteriorating life support for occupants
  • Structural collapse risk

Scenario 2: Safe Landing - Additional Protocols

Assumptions:

  • CRITICAL: Assume peaceful intent unless demonstrated otherwise
  • Beings capable of interstellar travel likely did not come to invade
  • Communication systems likely functional
  • Occupants prepared for interaction

Priority Actions:

  • WAIT and OBSERVE - Let aliens make first move
  • Listen for communication attempts
  • Maintain non-threatening posture
  • Prepare communication teams immediately
  • Document all actions and signals from craft

What NOT to Do:

  • Do not approach uninvited
  • Do not make sudden movements
  • Do not aim weapons (defensive positions only)
  • Do not use loud noises or bright lights aggressively
  • Do not attempt to board craft

Phase 3: Biological & Technological Containment (6-24 Hours)

üß¨ Biological Protocols

Biohazard Containment

ASSUME UNKNOWN PATHOGENS: No direct contact without Level A or Level B hazmat suits with SCBA (Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus).

For Biological Aliens:

  • Deploy mobile quarantine laboratories
  • Collect biological samples using remote or robotic systems where possible
  • Test for cross-species microbial compatibility before ANY treatment
  • Monitor for airborne pathogens in surrounding area
  • Establish quarantine perimeter (CDC pandemic protocols)

For Human-Appearing Aliens:

  • Maintain full hazmat protocols
  • Assume different biochemistry despite appearance
  • Test for compatibility before physical contact
  • Monitor for allergic or immune responses

Medical Ethics for Injured Aliens

  • Defer treatment to xenobiologists and bioethicists
  • Establish communication before attempting intervention
  • Avoid invasive procedures until consent established
  • Consider alien medical needs may be radically different
  • Document all medical decisions and ethical considerations

Human Health Monitoring

  • Document vaccination status of all responders
  • Establish decontamination stations
  • Prepare medical isolation facilities
  • Psychological support for witnesses (trauma response)
  • Long-term health monitoring of all exposed individuals

Technology Safety Protocols

TREAT ALL ALIEN TECHNOLOGY AS POTENTIALLY HAZARDOUS: Unknown power sources, AI systems, defensive mechanisms, or self-destruct capabilities may be present.

Isolation Procedures:

  • Use Faraday cages for electronic components
  • EM shielding for all removed devices
  • Prohibit civilian or unauthorized handling of debris
  • Scan for radiation, unusual energy emissions
  • Document power sources and propulsion systems

Sample Collection:

  • Collect ship hull material samples
  • Soil samples from impact/landing area
  • Atmospheric samples near craft
  • Any visible fuel or propellant residue
  • Document all collection with chain of custody
